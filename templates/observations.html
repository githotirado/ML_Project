Project Observations

Participant Demographics

Participants in the research had to provide consent to let their responses be used in the data set. More introverts than the other two groups both participated in the study and gave their consent, despite the general consensus that extroverts outnumber introverts by 3 to 1. For all distributions, the proportions of participant types remained consistent with majority of introverts over ambiverts and extroverts.

Figure 1

The overall distribution chart is based on how participants identified themselves. Since we know that in general extroverts outnumber introverts by 3 to 1, we might surmise that extroverts are much less inclined to participate in these research projects. As a result, fewer samples were available from extroverts to improve accuracy for identifying extroverts.

The Countries List displays the electronic location of the participants. The location data was not explicitly asked of the participants; researchers extrapolated location based on IP information. With the US, Great Britain, and Canada comprising the top 3 participant countries, it would be safe to assume that we should not use country data to train our model since this distribution does not adequately represent the true population density worldwide.

The age group of participants was mostly GenZ followed by Millenials, and more females participated than males. We excluded demographic data from the training and testing of our model because the distribution of participants did not mirror the world population. For example, the researchers used a responder's IP address to determine the countries where the questionnaires were responded; this metric as well did not reflect the population distribution of those countries.

Machine Learning

The Confusion Matrix [Figure 2] told a lot about the model's ability to predict the three personality traits. The model had the most success with predicting introverts, which was the largest group of participants. There were fewer self-identified extroverts in the data set to help us train the model to predict them. The largest group to contain false predictions was the ambivert group. This might be attributable to ambiverts with introvert functions and ambiverts with extrovert functions. The questionnaire only offered 3 choices for self identification. For example:
  • (Count=330) Where the participant identified as ambivert but was predicted to be introvert. This might be the ambiverts with introvert functions in our Personality Continuum Scale.
  • (Count=108) Where the participant identified as extrovert but was predicted to be ambivert. This might be the ambiverts with extrovert functions in our Personality Continuum Scale.

Question Responses

Some questions drew stronger responses than others for each type. The following table displays the questions with strong reactions.
