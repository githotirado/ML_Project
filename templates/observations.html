Project Observations

Participant Demographics

Participants in the research had to provide consent to let their responses be used in the data set. More introverts than the other two groups both participated in the study and gave their consent, despite the general consensus that extroverts outnumber introverts by 3 to 1.

The age group of participants was mostly GenZ followed by Millenials, and more females participated than males. We excluded demographic data from the training and testing of our model because the distribution of participants did not mirror the population demographics of the world. For example, the researchers used a responder's IP address to determine the countries where the questionnaires were responded; this metric as well did not reflect the population distribution of those countries. Observe the dashboard of responder demographics:

The overall distribution chart is based on how participants identified themselves. Since we know that in general extroverts outnumber introverts by 3 to 1, we might surmise that introverts are more inclined to participate in these research projects than extroverts. As a result, fewer samples were available from extroverts to improve accuracy for identifying extroverts.

The countries list displays the electronic location of the participants. With the US, Great Britain, and Canada comprising the top 3 participant countries, it would be safe to assume that we should not use country data to train our model since this distribution does not adequately represent the true population distribution.

Finally, the age and gender distributions show that mostly female and mostly GenZ people participated in the research. For all distributions, self-identified introverts were the largest participation groups.

Machine Learning

The Confusion Matrix proved to be telling about the model's ability to predict the three personality traits. The model had the most success with predicting introverts, which was the largest group of participants. There were fewer self-identified extroverts in the data set to help us train the model to predict them. The largest group to contain false predictions was the ambivert group. This might be attributable to ambiverts with introvert functions and ambiverts with extrovert functions. The questionnaire only offered 3 choices for self identification. For example:
  • (Count=330) Where the participant identified as ambivert but was predicted to be introvert. This might be the group in our Personality Continuum Scale as those ambiverts with introvert functions.
  • (Count=108) Where the participant identified as extrovert but was predicted to be ambivert. This might be the group in our Personality Continuum Scale as those ambiverts with extrovert funtions.

Question Responses

Some questions produced stronger responses than others for each trait. Introverts favor 1 on 1 interactions and spending time with their hobbies and prefer listening to people in conversations. Extroverts admitted they love to laugh a lot, stand up for themselves, love excitement, and have strong personalities. Ambiverts were neutral for most questions but were in strong agreement to loving excitement and laughing a lot.
